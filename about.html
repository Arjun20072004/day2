Early Computing Devices

The history of computers began with ancient calculating devices like the abacus (around 2700-2300 BC) and the Antikythera mechanism from ancient Greece.

Mechanical Era

In the 17th century, Blaise Pascal invented the Pascaline, an early mechanical calculator. Charles Babbage designed the Analytical Engine in the 1830s, considered the first general-purpose computer concept.

Electronic Computers

The ENIAC (Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer), built in 1946, was the first general-purpose electronic computer. It used vacuum tubes and weighed about 30 tons.

Modern Era

The invention of the transistor in 1947 revolutionized computing. Personal computers emerged in the 1970s-1980s with the Apple II, Commodore 64, and IBM PC. The internet era began in the 1990s, followed by mobile computing and cloud technology.

Contemporary Computing

Today, computers are ubiquitous in artificial intelligence, quantum computing, and advanced data processing, fundamentally transforming society and technology.
